How to review subtitles
Everything here saves automatically as you go.

The timeline strip at the top

  • Drag any subtitle to slide the whole timeline together — everything moves the same amount, so the shape stays intact.
  • Drag the left or right edge of a subtitle to change its length. If there's empty space next to it, the edge moves into that space. Once it touches the neighbor, they stay locked together and dragging further shortens the neighbor.
  • Hold Shift while dragging (either the body or an edge) to move only that one subtitle — neighbors won't move, and gaps or overlaps are allowed.
  • Click empty space on the timeline to jump the playhead there.
  • Drag empty space to select several subtitles at once (you'll see a blue box). Then drag any one of them to move the whole group together.

Playing the video

  • Press ▶ Play at the top or hit Space to play/pause.
  • Click ▶ Play this line on any subtitle to jump to its start and play from there.

Adjusting timing on one subtitle

  • Set Start / Set End — snaps the value to wherever the video is right now.
  • −0.1s / +0.1s — nudges the value by one-tenth of a second at a time, for fine tuning.

Editing the text

  • Click into any subtitle's text box and type. Changes save automatically.
  • Highlight some text and right-click to make it Bold or Italic.
  • Press Shift+Enter to add a line break inside a subtitle.

Adding, removing, splitting, and merging

  • Split here — cuts a subtitle in two at wherever your text cursor is.
  • Merge with next — combines this subtitle with the one after it.
  • Add line below — inserts a new empty subtitle after this one.
  • Delete — removes this subtitle.
  • Made a mistake? Press Ctrl+Z (or ⌘+Z on Mac) to undo. This works for every change.

Leaving notes

  • Click 💬 Notes on any subtitle to write a comment about it. Anyone else sharing this project will see your notes.

Saving

There's no Save button — every edit is written to the cloud automatically. You can close the tab whenever you're done.
